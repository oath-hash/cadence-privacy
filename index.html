Cadence Privacy Policy

Effective date: 28 May 2026. For the Cadence iOS app.

Summary. Cadence stores its data on your device and does not send it anywhere. It does not collect, transmit, share, or sell data. It has no servers, no analytics, no third-party tracking, and no advertising. Your reminders and progress stay on your phone.

1. Scope

This policy describes how the Cadence iOS app handles information. It applies to the Cadence app only.

It does not cover Apple's handling of data when you install, update, or use an app from the App Store. See Apple's Privacy Policy for that.

2. How the app handles data

Cadence stores its data on the device. There is no Cadence server, and the app does not make network requests. Reminders, settings, and usage stay on the device and are not visible to the developer or any third party.

The sections below cover each data category in Apple's App Privacy questionnaire and each function of the app.

3. Data the app does not collect

Apple's App Privacy questionnaire lists categories an app might collect. Cadence collects, transmits, or shares none of the following:

  • Contact info (name, email address, phone number, physical address, other user contact info).
  • Health and fitness (health, fitness, or wellness data of any kind).
  • Financial info (payment info, credit info, other financial info).
  • Location (precise location, coarse location).
  • Sensitive info (racial or ethnic data, sexual orientation, pregnancy or childbirth info, disability, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, political opinion, genetic information, biometric data).
  • Contacts (your address book).
  • User content (emails or messages, photos or videos, audio data, gameplay content, customer support, other user content). Text entered into a reminder is stored on the device only. See Section 4.
  • Browsing history.
  • Search history.
  • Identifiers (user ID, device ID, advertising identifiers). Cadence does not assign or read any persistent identifier for any purpose.
  • Purchases (purchase history).
  • Usage data (product interaction, advertising data, other usage data).
  • Diagnostics (crash data, performance data, other diagnostic data).
  • Other data.

Cadence contains no analytics, advertising, attribution, crash-reporting, or other third-party SDK that sends data.

4. Data the app stores on the device

Cadence stores the following on the device only:

  • Reminders. Title and message text, the operating-hours window, the interval, the active weekdays, and tracking settings such as a daily target and catch-up mode. Stored in a local SwiftData database in the app's sandboxed container.
  • Daily progress. For a reminder with tracking enabled, a per-day count of logged amounts, used to show how much remains and to stop reminders once the target is reached. Same container.
  • App preferences and state. Small settings such as first-launch state, stored in iOS preferences (UserDefaults) in the same container.

This data is not transmitted and is not stored on any Cadence server, because there is no Cadence server. If iCloud Backup is enabled on the device, iOS may include this data in the device's encrypted iCloud Backup. That is handled by Apple and is covered by Apple's privacy policy.

Deleting the app removes the app's container and its contents.

5. Notifications and permissions

Cadence uses notifications, so it requests permission to send them. The request appears when you enable your first reminder, not at launch. If permission is declined, the app shows an explanation and a link to the Settings screen. No other permission is requested.

Reminders are scheduled on the device by iOS. The schedule and the reminder text are not sent anywhere. Notification actions such as Log set, Done for today, and Skip update the local progress count and reschedule the remaining reminders on the device.

Cadence does not request access to camera, microphone, photo library, location, contacts, calendar, the system Reminders database, motion data, HealthKit, HomeKit, Bluetooth, local network, App Tracking Transparency, Siri, or speech recognition.

6. Children

Cadence is not directed specifically at children under 13. The app collects no data, so no additional data handling applies to users under 13.

7. Your rights

Data-protection laws such as GDPR and the UK Data Protection Act provide rights to access, correct, export, and delete personal data held about you. Cadence holds no personal data on any server, so there is nothing held by Cadence to act on. Data on the device can be edited or deleted within the app, and removed entirely by deleting the app.

8. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated. Changes that affect what data is handled will be noted in the App Store release notes for the relevant version, and this page will be updated.
